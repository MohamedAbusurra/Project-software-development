## Full Script

---

### INTRODUCTION

Imagine you have an idea for an app.
A great one.
But before a single user can try it, you need to buy servers,
set them up in a data center,
and somehow guess how much computing power you'll need -
before you even know if anyone cares.

That was the reality for every software team before 2006.
Today, we're talking about the technology that changed all of that:
**Infrastructure as a Service** - and how Amazon Web Services made it possible.

---

### WHAT IS IAAS

Infrastructure as a Service, or IaaS, means renting the core building blocks of computing
instead of buying them.
That includes computing power, storage, and networking -
everything you'd normally need physical servers for -
delivered over the internet, on demand.

To put it in perspective:
IaaS is like renting an empty plot of land with power and water already connected.
You build what you want on top.
Compare that to SaaS - Software as a Service - which is more like moving into a fully built house.
Gmail, Google Docs - those are SaaS.
IaaS gives you the foundation; you decide what to build.

---

### HOW AWS CHANGED EVERYTHING

In 2006, Amazon Web Services launched two products that kicked off the cloud computing market:
S3 for storage, and EC2 for computing power.

Before this, building software meant a massive upfront cost -
buying hardware before the business had earned a single dollar.
AWS flipped that model.
Instead of a huge capital expense, you paid month to month, only for what you used.

The impact was immediate.
Netflix rebuilt its entire streaming platform on AWS after a near-fatal database crash in 2008.
Instagram scaled to 13 million users in under a year - with just 13 people -
because they never had to own a single server.

AWS didn't just lower costs. It removed the barrier between an idea and a global product.

---

### WHY IT STILL MATTERS TODAY

Almost twenty years later, IaaS isn't slowing down.
The global market was around $175 billion in 2025
and is expected to grow to nearly $200 billion in 2026 -
and keep climbing toward $330 billion by 2030.

AWS is still the leader, holding about 33% of cloud infrastructure spending.
Microsoft Azure sits at 23%, and Google Cloud at 12%.
Together, those three control roughly two-thirds of the entire market.

And the biggest driver of that growth right now?
Artificial intelligence.
Every AI model being trained, every chatbot answering questions -
it all runs on rented compute.
The same basic idea AWS launched with in 2006
is now powering the AI boom of 2026.

---

### CONCLUSION

IaaS started as a simpler way to rent a server.
It became the foundation the modern software industry is built on.

From startups launching with no hardware budget,
to AI companies training billion-parameter models -
it all runs on cloud infrastructure.

Thanks for watching. We're Group 3, and this has been our look at AWS and the Rise of IaaS.
