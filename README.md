## 📌 Topic Summary

Before 2006, launching software meant buying physical servers,
waiting weeks for hardware, and hoping nothing crashed.
Scaling to millions of users was a problem only big companies
could afford to solve.

AWS changed that. By offering rent-by-the-hour computing
over the internet, IaaS made massive infrastructure accessible
to anyone. Netflix rebuilt its entire streaming platform on AWS
after a 2008 database crash nearly killed the company.
Instagram scaled to 13 million users in under a year —
with a team of 13 people — because they never had to
own a single server.

That's the real story of IaaS: it didn't just cut costs,
it removed the barrier between an idea and a global product.
